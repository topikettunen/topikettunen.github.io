Reawakening habit of writing during times of quarantine

A couple of years ago I had a good habit of writing semi-regularly about various topics that were interesting to me. These included the first impressions of different technologies, my opinions on different topics, etc. Unfortunately, time passed and I began to write less and less and recently I’ve gotten out of the habit completely. Which is a shame in many ways since I’ve always felt writing to be greatly therapeutic.

At the time of writing this world is also in a very odd place. Most of the countries around the world are quarantined due to COVID-19 and people are staying in their homes intending to flatten the curve (see https://www.flattenthecurve.com/). Yours truly included! So as a way to pass the time during these times I’m trying to reawaken this habit.

Most of the topics that I would like to write about are relevant to my everyday life, which is mainly revolving around tech and music, and quite often a combination of these two. Currently, I’m working in the field of DevOps and Site Reliability Engineering (SRE) so all things cloud-native and distributed systems are close to my heart. Although my main interest in the world of computer science lies in the world of programming languages, compilers, interpreters and runtimes with a hint of software and language design.

My free time is usually spent with various open-source projects, composing music and running my own independent records label so obviously, all thing music is also close to my heart. Lately, I’ve also been into Digital Signal Processing (DSP) and Virtual Studio Technology (VST), mainly for the reason of this quarantine. Diving into this rabbit hole has been very rewarding for me since this is something that combines my interest in programming to music.

Like I said earlier, writing has always been greatly therapeutic and beneficial for me. I’ve seen it can help me with my learning of different things since I can contemplate these learnings through writing. While staying in quarantine, I shouldn’t have any excuses for not writing, so I try to maintain a healthy and regular pace in it. Hopefully, I’m able to find the old habit and then maintain it long into the future.
